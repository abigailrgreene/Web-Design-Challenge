The visualization to the right is representing the relationship between City Latitude and Max Temperature, that is, the relationship between proximity to the equator and peak temperature, for 500+ cities. With the exception of a few outliers, this data shows that peak temperatures tend to increase as the distance from the equator decreases. The closer a specific city is located to the equator, the more likely it is to have a higher peak temperature than a city further from the equator.

This data was obtained from the OpenWeatherMap API.
